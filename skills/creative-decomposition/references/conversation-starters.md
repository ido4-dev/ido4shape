# Conversation Starters by Project Type

Opening hooks and follow-up patterns adapted to different types of work.

## User-Feature Projects

**Opening:**
"Let's start with the human side of this problem. Tell me about the last time you — or someone on your team — personally experienced this frustration."

**Follow-ups after problem description:**
- "That sounds genuinely frustrating. How long have people been dealing with this?"
- "What workarounds have people built? Those are often the best requirement signals."
- "Who suffers most from this — and is it the same person who'd benefit most from a solution?"

**Follow-ups after user mention:**
- "Tell me about [user type] — what makes their situation unique?"
- "Walk me through their typical workflow right now. Where does it break?"

**Follow-ups after solution idea:**
- "What assumptions are you making about how users will actually behave?"
- "If we built exactly this, what's the simplest version that would still solve the core problem?"

## Tech-Debt Projects

**Opening:**
"Technical debt is fascinating because there's always a story of how it accumulated. Take me on a tour of this system — what makes you cringe when you look at it?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "Tell me about the last time this tech debt caused a real problem in production."
- "If you had to explain to a new developer why this is problematic, what would you show them first?"
- "How much time is your team spending working around this issue vs. building new things?"
- "What's the scariest part of touching this code?"

## Migration Projects

**Opening:**
"Let's understand the journey. Paint me a picture: what does the current state look like vs. where you want to be?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "What would happen if you didn't migrate — what's the real cost of staying put?"
- "What keeps you up at night about this migration?"
- "Have you done similar migrations before? What surprised you?"
- "What's the rollback plan if something goes wrong halfway through?"

## Integration Projects

**Opening:**
"I'm curious about what triggered this integration need. What business process is currently broken without this connection?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "Tell me about the system you're integrating with — what's it like to work with?"
- "How reliable is their API? Have you seen the documentation?"
- "What's the data flow — who's the source of truth when systems disagree?"

## Bug-Fix Projects

**Opening:**
"Bug hunting! Walk me through what should happen vs. what actually happens."

**Follow-ups:**
- "Can you reproduce it consistently? What triggers it?"
- "How many users does this affect? Is it everyone or a specific segment?"
- "When did it start? What changed around that time?"
- "What have you already tried?"

## Enhancement Projects

**Opening:**
"Tell me about the gap between what users can do today and what they wish they could do."

**Follow-ups:**
- "Is this driven by user requests, or is this something you've identified as a gap?"
- "What's the existing feature doing well that we should preserve?"
- "If we enhance this, what breaks? What existing workflows need to adapt?"

## Stakeholder-Specific Openers (Multi-Stakeholder Sessions)

### Architect Joining an Existing Project

**Opening:**
"I've been working with the PM on [project]. Quick context: [2-sentence problem]. The solution shape is forming around [key concepts]. I have three technical questions, and I'd rather get your honest take before the architecture hardens."

**Follow-ups:**
- "Is this achievable with your current infrastructure, or does it need new capabilities?"
- "What's the scariest part of this technically?"
- "Where would you draw the architectural boundaries differently from what we have?"

### UX Designer Joining an Existing Project

**Opening:**
"We've been shaping a [project type] spec. The problem is [1 sentence]. The engineers have proposed [key interaction points]. I want your perspective before these solidify — specifically on [2-3 specific UI/UX questions]."

**Follow-ups:**
- "Walk me through what a user would actually see and do here."
- "The engineer version of this flow has [N] steps — does that feel right from a user perspective?"
- "What happens when this fails? What should the user experience?"

### Business Stakeholder Joining

**Opening:**
"We're shaping the scope for [project]. The team has a solution direction, but I want to make sure the business constraints are right before we lock it down. Two things I need from you: timeline reality and budget implications."

**Follow-ups:**
- "The current scope implies roughly [effort range]. Does that match the business expectation?"
- "Are there organizational dependencies — other teams, approvals, compliance gates?"
- "What's the competitive pressure here — are we racing someone?"

### Returning Stakeholder (Same Person, New Session)

**Opening (if new materials were processed):**
"Since last time, I processed [what was added]. [Specific observation]. This connects to [something from their previous session]."

**Opening (if tensions need resolution):**
"There's a tension I've been holding since your last session and [other stakeholder]'s input. [Describe tension]. I think we're ready to resolve it now."

**Opening (if dimension is thin):**
"Looking at where we are — I feel solid about [strong areas]. But [thin dimension] needs more depth. That's where I'd like to focus today."

## Universal Recovery Patterns

When conversation energy drops:

**Re-energize:** "You know what? We're building something really solid here. Look at everything we've figured out together — [list key insights]. The remaining unknowns are actually quite focused."

**Refocus:** "Let me step back and make sure I understand the big picture. Here's what I think we're building: [summary]. Am I reading this right?"

**Find their passion:** "What part of this project excites you most? Let's dig into that for a while."

**Acknowledge fatigue:** "We've covered a lot of ground. Want to pause here and pick up the [thinnest dimension] next time?"
